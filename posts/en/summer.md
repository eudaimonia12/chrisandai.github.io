A stretch of lawn where few people go, a great tree overhead for shade, and cicadas at eighty decibels in your ears.

You lie flat on the ground as if struck down, playing "Summertime Sadness" over a speaker — a song you've listened to since junior high.

Every summer, you have to sink into your own fantasies; the hot, idle hours seem to offer a doorway, and you can return again and again to childhood.

But you know those fantasies matter to you.
